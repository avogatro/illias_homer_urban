Urban Slang Edition • Book 18

The News Drops

Antilochus ran to Achilles. He was crying. He couldn't look Achilles in the face.

"Yo, Patroclus..." he choked out. "He's gone. The Trojans wrecked him."

Achilles went cold. Then he exploded.

He grabbed dust and poured it over his head. He fell on the ground and wailed. His scream was so loud it shook the tent.

His mom, Thetis, heard it from the deep ocean. She started screaming too. Her sisters came to comfort her.

Thetis & Achilles

Thetis flew out of the water. She found Achilles holding Patroclus' body.

"Why are you crying, son? You're doomed to die young. I know that. But you're still here," she said.

"Hector killed him," Achilles sobbed. "Patroclus took my armor. I didn't help him. I'm gonna kill Hector. Then I'll die myself. I don't care."

"But I have no armor!" he said. "Hector has my gear."

"Don't worry," Thetis said. "I'll get you new stuff. Better stuff. Don't go out yet. Wait."

The Pushback

While the gods were talking, the fight over the body was getting ugly.

Hector was trying to drag the body to Troy, but Menelaus rallied the Greeks.

"You really think you're gonna drag him to the city?" Menelaus yelled. "Not on my watch."

Ajax the Great started throwing rocks. He killed a Trojan leader named Hippothous.

The Trojans got scared. They couldn't even touch the body without getting speared.

Apollo stepped in to protect Hector. "Get back, Hector!" he whispered. "The Greeks are crazy right now."

The Blacksmith

Thetis went to Hephaestus, the god of fire and metalworking.

She found him sweating over his forge. He was making robots for his shop.

"Son," she said. "My boy needs armor. Now."

Hephaestus was down. "Your boy saved my life once. I owe you."

He started working. He put gold, tin, silver, and bronze into the fire.

The Shield of Achilles

Hephaestus made a shield. It wasn't just a shield. It was a masterpiece. He basically painted the world on it.

  • The Cosmos: Earth, sky, sea, sun, moon, and all the stars.
  • Two Cities: One having a wedding and a court case (Peace). One being raided and soldiers fighting (War).
  • The Farm: Plowmen working the fields, reapers cutting wheat, kids stealing grapes.
  • The Animals: Herds of cows being attacked by lions, shepherds and dogs trying to fight back.
  • The Dance: A dance floor with a kid playing the lyre and couples dancing in a circle.

He made the armor shiny and gold. He handed it to Thetis.

She flew back down to earth. "Suit up," she told Achilles. "It's time."
