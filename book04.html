Urban Slang Edition • Book 4

The God Meeting

Up on Olympus, the gods were chilling. Zeus looked down at the battlefield.

"Yo, this truce is beautiful," he said. "Maybe we should just end the war now. Let Menelaus keep his win."

Hera, who absolutely hates the Trojans, was not having it. She rolled her eyes.

"Are you serious?" she snapped. "You just gonna let them off the hook? All my effort wasted?"

Zeus knew better than to argue. "Fine, fine. You win. Do what you want. But expect me to be mad."

Athena's Scheme

Hera gathered the troops. She told Athena, "Go down there. Make sure the Trojans break the truce first. We need a reason to crash out on them."

Athena was down. She transformed herself to look like a Trojan soldier. She found a guy named Pandarus.

This Pandarus was a sniper. He had a crazy bow. Athena whispered in his ear.

"Hey, pretty boy," she lied. "Look at Menelaus standing there like a boss. If you snipe him right now, you’ll be famous. You’ll get so much clout."

Pandarus was dumb enough to listen.

The Cheap Shot

Pandarus pulled out his bow. It was made from antelope horns—top-tier gear. He prayed to Apollo.

He took aim. He released the string. The arrow flew fast and silent.

*Thwack.*

It hit Menelaus. It didn't kill him, thank the gods. It went through his gear and grazed his skin, drawing blood. But it looked way worse than it was.

Agamemnon Freaks Out

When Agamemnon saw his brother hit, he panicked.

"Oh my god! He's dead! The truce is broken! This is exactly why you can't trust Trojans!"

He was ready to burn the whole place down. "We’re going to lose this war without him!"

The Doctor

But the medic, Machaon, stepped up. "Hold up," he said. "Let me check him."

He examined the wound. He pulled out the arrow. He sucked out the blood and applied some special ointment.

"He's good," Machaon said. "It's just a scratch. The armor saved him."

Menelaus got back up, ready to fight. The army was hyped again.

The Hype Speech

Agamemnon took the mic. He started roasting his own troops to get them motivated.

"Look at you cowards," he yelled. "You act tough, but you just wanna run away. If you leave now, you go home broke."

Then he praised the kings. He went to Odysseus. "You keep it real, son. You don't talk much, but when you fight, you fight."

He went to Agamemnon (himself) and Ajax. "These guys are beasts."

He even roasted Idomeneus a little. Just to keep everyone on their toes.

The Clash

The horns blew. *TOOOOT. TOOOOT.*

The Greeks marched forward like a tidal wave. The Trojans screamed back and charged.

They collided in the middle of the plain. Shields crashed against spears. It was a brawl.

Blood started flowing immediately. It was chaos. No more truce. It was straight murder.

Simoisius, a young Trojan, got killed by Ajax. He didn't even know what hit him.

Antilochus, a Greek, killed a Trojan guy named Echepolus with a rock to the head. Straight up dropped him.

The bodies started piling up. Dust covered everyone. The war was back on.
