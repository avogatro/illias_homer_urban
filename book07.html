Urban Slang Edition • Book 7

Hector Wants Smoke

Zeus was watching the fight. He was bored. He took his golden scales—the ones that measure fate—and tipped it.

The scale landed on Hector. That meant Hector was the man of the hour.

Hector shouted to the Greeks. "Listen up! I’m done killing randoms. I want a fair one. Bring out your best fighter. 1v1. Let's do it."

The Greeks went quiet. Nobody wanted to step up. Hector was scary.

Menelaus was about to go, but Agamemnon grabbed him. "Chill, bro. You love fighting, but Hector is a beast. You might get cooked."

Ajax Steps Up

Finally, big Ajax stepped forward. He stood there like a tank. He was the only one confident enough.

"I'll do it," Ajax said. "I'm not scared of Hector."

He put on his helmet. He looked like a walking mountain. The Greeks were hyped.

Hector and Ajax walked to the middle of the no-man's-land. They circled each other like boxers.

•

The 12-Round Brawl

Hector threw his spear first. It hit Ajax’s shield but didn't go through. It bounced off.

Ajax threw his. It went through Hector's shield and grazed his neck, drawing blood. But it didn't drop him.

They were like, "Okay, spears are done."

They picked up huge rocks. They were hurling boulders at each other like crazy people.

Then they drew swords. They hacked at each other until sunset. It was a stalemate. Both guys were tired, sweating, but still standing.

Respect

The referees saw the sun going down. They blew the whistle.

"Stop! Good game."

Hector reached out his hand. "You're built different, Ajax. You're strong as a bull. Let's be cool."

Ajax gave him a fist bump. "You too, man. You fight like a god. Best I've ever seen."

They exchanged gifts. Hector gave Ajax his sword (with a silver handle). Ajax gave Hector his belt (and armor). No hard feelings.

The Trojan Huddle

The next day, the Trojans met up. They were scared of Diomedes.

Antenor stood up. "Yo, let's just give Helen back. And the loot. This war is draining us."

Paris stood up. "Nah. I won't give her back. That’s weak. But I'll give back all the loot I stole. Plus extra."

Priam, the King, was like, "Alright, I'll send a messenger. Let's tell the Greeks. We want peace."

The Greek Huddle

The messenger told the Greeks the deal. Agamemnon listened.

Then Diomedes interrupted. "Stop! Don't listen to him."

"Even if they give Helen back, don't trust 'em. Their hearts are crooked. Let's just fight until Troy falls."

The army cheered. They wanted to keep fighting.

The Truce & The Wall

They agreed to a one-day truce just to burn their dead bodies. Respect to the fallen.

The Trojans did their thing. But the Greeks used the time smartly.

Nestor said, "We need defenses. If they attack our ships, we’re cooked."

They built a massive wall around their camp. Huge trenches. High gates. It was basically a fortress. Poseidon helped them build it fast.

The Trojans saw the wall and got nervous. They called a meeting in the city. They planned how to attack the Greek wall.
