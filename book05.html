The Iliad

Urban Slang Edition • Book 5

Diomedes Goes Beast Mode

Meet Diomedes. He’s a Greek king, and today is his day. He’s in the zone. He’s possessed by the spirit of war.

Athena is literally standing behind his chariot, hyping him up. She took the "fog" off his eyes so he can spot the gods on the battlefield.

Diomedes starts wrecking shop. He’s killing Trojan soldiers left and right. It looks like a horror movie. Bodies dropping everywhere.

Aeneas vs. Diomedes

Pandarus—that guy who shot the arrow in the last book—steps up. He says to Aeneas, "Yo, let's get Diomedes."

Pandarus throws a spear. Hits Diomedes' shoulder. But Diomedes is built different. He barely flinches.

Diomedes throws his spear back. *Kaboom.* It goes right through Pandarus' face. Game over for him.

Now it's Diomedes vs. Aeneas. Aeneas grabs a rock—he’s about to crush Diomedes. But Aeneas is a demigod son of Aphrodite, so he has backup.

•

Aphrodite Gets Cut

Aphrodite sees her son Aeneas is in trouble. She throws a cloud over him to hide him and grabs him to pull him out.

Diomedes sees the goddess. He doesn't care. "Get off the field, goddess!" he yells.

He lunges with his spear and cuts her wrist.

She screams. *Ichor*—that’s god blood—spills out. It looks gold. It hurts like crazy because gods are usually immortal.

Aphrodite drops Aeneas and runs away crying to Olympus. "Dad, did you see that? Diomedes just tried to scrap me!"

Apollo Intervenes

Apollo, Aeneas' other dad (long story), catches his son before he hits the ground.

He creates a fake body of Aeneas so the Greeks can fight over that while he teleports the real Aeneas back to his shrine to heal.

Diomedes, still hyped on rage, screams at Apollo. "I’m coming for you too!"

Apollo looks at him. "Nah, son. Don't be dumb. I'm an eternal god. Don't step to me."

Diomedes backs down. He knows his limits.

•

The Rivalry

Ares, the God of War, is on the Trojan side. He sees how hard Diomedes is killing his team. He decides to get involved disguised as a mortal.

Meanwhile, Athena finds Diomedes again. "I see you winning," she says. "I’m giving you a permanent buff. If Ares comes near, hit him. He’s a punk."

Diomedes vs. Ares

Ares and Diomedes meet. Ares throws a spear, Athena swats it away. Then Diomedes throws his spear. Athena guides it.

*THUNK.*

The spear hits Ares right in the gut. It goes deep. Ares lets out a scream that sounds like 10,000 soldiers yelling at once.

Ares drops his spear. He looks like he got jumped in an alley. He blasts off back to Olympus, terrified.

The Roast Session on Olympus

Ares stumbles in, bleeding god-blood. He sits next to Zeus and cries.

"Dad, look at this wound! Athena set me up!"

Zeus gives him the dirtiest look. "You're pathetic," Zeus says. "You love fighting, but you can't handle getting hit. You're the most annoying god I have."

Zeus tells the healers to patch Ares up. Hera and Athena are lowkey laughing.

The End of the Day

Back on Earth, Diomedes is a legend. The Trojans are terrified of him. They think he’s invincible.

The Trojans retreat back to their city walls. The Greeks are winning big time.
