Urban Slang Edition • Book 19

The Glow Up

Thetis brought the new gear to Achilles. She dropped it on the ground. It shone like a fire.

It was loud. The metal clanked. The Myrmidons (Achilles' squad) were scared to even look at it. They scattered.

But Achilles couldn't take his eyes off it. It was rage mixed with art.

He put it on. It fit perfectly. He felt powerful.

Then he screamed.

The Battle Cry

Thetis filled the air with nectar so he wouldn't starve too fast.

Achilles let out a roar. Athena added her voice to it.

It was terrifying. The Trojans heard it and froze. The Greeks heard it and felt sick.

The sound made 12 Trojans fall off their chariots just from the fright.

The Huddle

Achilles called the Greek leaders together. He didn't want to fight anymore. He wanted to squash the beef so they could focus on Hector.

"Agamemnon," Achilles said. "We fought over a girl. It was dumb. But I'm over it. Let's move."

"Are we sending the army out or nah?"

The "My Bad"

Agamemnon stood up from his seat. "Yo, listen everyone. It wasn't my fault. It was Zeus. He blinded me."

"I made a mistake. I want to fix it. I’ll give back the girl. I'll give the gifts. I'll throw in extra."

He called his boys. "Go get the loot. Go get Briseis. Bring it all here."

Odysseus Checks Him

Odysseus interrupted. "Chill, bro. Don't push the army away just to make a speech."

Let the men eat first. We can do the whole ceremony with the gifts later. We need to fight NOW."

Briseis Returns

The guards brought Briseis out. She walked toward Achilles.

But when she saw Patroclus’ body, she lost it.

She fell on the corpse and cried. "You were like a big brother to me," she sobbed. "Achilles is my husband, but you were nice to me. You treated me like a queen."

All the women wailed with her. Achilles joined in. It was a sad scene.

Zero Patience

Zeus was watching. He told Hera, "My son isn't eating. He wants revenge."

Hera said, "Let him starve if he wants. Or let Athena feed him. I don't care."

Athena flew down. She poured sweet nectar into Achilles' chest. He didn't even know she did it.

He strapped on his armor. He grabbed his massive spear.

"I'm done talking," he said. "I'm going for Hector."
