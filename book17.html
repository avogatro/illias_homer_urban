The Heist

Menealus saw Patroclus go down. "We can't lose the body," he yelled.

He stood over Patroclus like a guard dog. He killed anyone who tried to touch the gear.

But Hector was right there. He yelled to his squad: "Whoever gets the body gets the loot! Let's go!"

Hector stripped the armor off Patroclus. He was wearing Achilles' legendary gear now.

Zeus watched Hector put on the helmet. "You look cool," Zeus thought, "but that armor is cursed. It's actually leading to your grave."

The Roast

Glaucus, the Lycian captain, was heated. He stepped to Hector.

"Look at you, Hector. You claim you're the leader, but you act like a coward."

"You let Sarpedon's body get dragged away by the Greeks without saying a word. But now you're fighting hard for this guy? Why? Because you want his shiny armor? You're clout chasing."

Hector was angry but focused. "Don't test me. I fight for the win. Get your hands dirty."

The Mosh Pit

The fight turned into a straight-up scrum.

Ajax the Great came up. "Who touches the body gets wrecked!"

Athena also showed up in disguise to help the Greeks.

Zeus was like, "If my son Sarpedon is gone, I don't care if his body gets dragged. I don't care if they strip him."

But Zeus made sure Apollo saved Sarpedon's body and washed it clean earlier.

Now, it was all about Patroclus.

The Tug-of-War

The Greeks were losing. Menelaus knew he couldn't hold the body alone.

He ran to find Ajax. "Bro, help me. They're gonna take Patroclus to Troy."

Ajax ran back. He killed a Trojan leader named Hippothous. "Get off him!"

Then Menelaus dragged the body away. It was gruesome. Blood everywhere.

Apollo covered the battlefield in a thick fog. It was hard to see. It was chaos.

The Escape

Athena helped Ajax find his way through the fog. He kept killing Trojans who tried to grab the body.

Hector chased them, but he couldn't stop the Greek tank.

The Greeks managed to drag Patroclus' body back behind their wall.

It wasn't a win. It was just damage control.

Achilles was about to hear the news.
