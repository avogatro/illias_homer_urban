The Iliad

Urban Slang Edition • Book 6

Cold-Blooded

The battle kept raging. The gods were just chilling, watching humans destroy each other.

Diomedes was still in the zone. He took a Trojan named Adrestus alive. The guy was begging for his life.

"Please," he cried. "My dad is rich! He'll pay you huge ransom!"

Diomedes was about to listen, but Agamemnon cut in.

"No, son," Agamemnon said. "Don't get soft. If you let them go, they just come back to fight us. Leave no survivors."

So Diomedes did the dirty work. He killed the guy. Brutal.

The Trojan Plan

The Greeks were pushing hard. The Trojans were folding.

Helenus, a Trojan prophet, saw what was happening. He pulled Hector aside.

"Hector, listen. We losing bad. You need to go back to the city," he said.

"Tell our mom to gather the ladies. Tell them to go to Athena’s temple and offer her a fancy dress. Pray for mercy. Maybe she’ll chill out on us."

Back in Troy

Hector bounced from the fight. The Greeks tried to shoot arrows at him, but his armor was too thick.

He got to the city gates and met his mom, Hecuba.

"Ma, don't give me wine," Hector said. "I'm weak. I need to be sober. Go tell the ladies to sacrifice to Athena. Give her the best dress in your closet."

Hecuba did exactly that. She told the Trojan women to bring an offering.

They prayed hard. But Athena left them on read. She wasn't interested.

The Paris Roast

Hector went to find his brother Paris. He found him at home, polishing his armor like a model.

Hector was heated. "So you here playing dress-up while our boys die out there?" he yelled.

"The war is your fault, bro. You stole the girl, now you can't even fight for her?"

Paris looked guilty. "My bad, Hector," he said. "I wasn't trying to slack. I was waiting for my squad. I'll go fight now."

Hector calmed down. "Good. Come on. We gonna need you."

The Goodbye

Before going back to war, Hector stopped to see his wife, Andromache.

She wasn't at home. She ran to the city wall to look for him.

When she saw him, she burst into tears.

"You're too brave, Hector," she said. "You're gonna get yourself killed. Then the Greeks will take our son, Astyanax, and throw him off the walls. Or make him a slave. I'll be a widow. It’s too much."

The Baby

Hector took off his helmet. His wife was scared of it. He held his son.

But the baby started crying. The helmet was scary, and the plume on top made him nervous.

Hector laughed. He took the helmet off and set it on the ground. He kissed his baby.

Then he prayed. "Zeus, let this boy be a better man than me. Let him be strong. Let him bring joy to his mom."

The Motivation

Hector looked at his wife. "I know, babe. I worry too. But I can't just chill inside."

"If I act like a coward, what will people say? If I don't fight, I'm dishonoring myself and my ancestors."

"My heart hurts thinking about you getting dragged away by Greeks. But I have to do this."

He reached for his baby, but the kid clung to his mom’s dress. Hector smiled, touched his son's hair, and put his helmet back on.

He walked away. Andromache watched him go, crying like she was already at his funeral.

Hector went back to the gates. The war was waiting.
