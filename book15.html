Urban Slang Edition • Book 15

The Hangover

Zeus woke up. He saw the Trojans were losing big time.

He saw Hector on the ground, gasping for air. He saw Poseideon helping the Greeks.

He looked at Hera. "I see you," he said. "You played me. You distracted me with the vibes."

Hera tried to play innocent. "I didn't do anything!"

Zeus wasn't having it. "You're lucky I don't end you right now. But we got rules. You gotta watch your mouth."

Getting the Squad in Line

Zeus ordered Hera to call the other gods.

"Tell them if they help the Trojans or Greeks today, I'm throwing them into the deepest pit. No cap."

Then he turned to Apollo. "You're too busy feeling sorry for Hector. Get back in the game."

Then he called Poseidon. "Get out. Go home to the sea. Unless you want to fight me."

Poseideon was salty. "Zeus, I'm your brother! You acting high and mighty."

Zeus threatened him again. Poseideon sighed. "Fine. I dip." He walked away into the ocean.

Hector's 2.0 Update

Zeus turned back to Apollo. "Go heal Hector. Give him strength. Make the Greeks run again."

Apollo flew down. He poured power into Hector.

Hector got up like he was glitching. He felt fresh. He put his armor back on.

The Trojans cheered when they saw their boss back on his feet.

The Greeks saw it too and their stomachs dropped.

The Rush to the Sea

Teucer, a Greek archer, tried to snipe Hector. But Hector threw a rock and smashed his collarbone again.

"Nobody can stop me!" Hector yelled.

The Greeks broke formation. They ran screaming from the wall all the way to the beach.

They were fighting literally by the water's edge now.

•

The Fire

Patroclus (Achilles' bestie) saw the smoke. The Trojans were setting their ships on fire.

He ran back to Achilles. "Man, look," he said. "Our boys are getting cooked."

Achilles was still salty. "So?"

"If they burn the ships, we can't go home," Patroclus said. "Let me take your armor. Maybe if they see 'Achilles' coming out, they'll freeze."

Achilles thought about it. "Alright. But don't get cocky. Just save the ships and come back."

But it was too late. The Trojans were at the front door.

End of Book 15
