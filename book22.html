The Iliad

Urban Slang Edition • Book 22

The Trick

The Trojans were hiding inside the city walls. Only Hector was left outside.

Apollo, who was posing as a Trojan, appeared to Hector.

"Hey, why are you chasing Achilles? You're just running in circles," Apollo said.

But then Apollo revealed himself. "Peace out," he said, and vanished.

Hector realized he had been played. He stood there, alone.

The Plea

Old King Priam saw Achilles walking toward the city. He saw the look in Achilles' eyes. It was the look of death.

"Hector!" Priam screamed from the wall. "Please, come inside! You're the only hope left for this city. Don't throw your life away!"

His mom, Hecuba, started crying. "She showed her breast. "Hector, have mercy on me! Don't let him kill you!"

But Hector stood firm. He couldn't leave. He was too embarrassed.

The Chase

Achilles got closer. Hector's courage faded.

"Oh snap," Hector thought. "I'm done for."

He dropped his shield and turned to run. Achilles took off after him.

They didn't run in a straight line. They ran around the city walls. Three full laps.

It wasn't a race. It was a hunt. Achilles was a cheetah. Hector was a stag trying to escape.

The Gods' Commentary

Zeus was watching. "Should we save Hector?" he asked the other gods.

"He's a good guy. He burns sacrifices to us."

Athena snapped. "Dad, stop. Hector is dead meat. His time is up."

Zeus agreed. "Bet. Do what you want."

•

The Fake Out

Hector was exhausted. Achilles was like the Terminator—he never got tired.

Athena appeared to Hector in the form of his brother, Deiphobus.

"Bro!" the fake brother said. "Let's stand together. We can take him."

Hector was hyped. He stopped running.

"Deiphobus, you’re the real MVP for standing with me," Hector said.

The Final Stand

Hector yelled at Achilles. "I'm done running. Let's end this. We agree, winner strips the loser's armor and gives the body back. No disrespect."

Achilles laughed. "Are you high? I can't promise you that. I'm gonna kill you, strip you, and let the dogs eat you."

He threw his spear. It went right through Hector's neck.

The End

Hector fell. "I see you now," he whispered. "You tricked me, Athena. You weren't my brother."

"Die happy," Achilles said. "They can't save you."

Hector died. His soul fled to Hades.

Achilles stripped Hector of his armor. The good armor—the armor he stole from Patroclus.

The Dragging

Achilles pierced Hector's heels with leather straps. He tied the ropes to his chariot.

He whipped the horses. The chariot zoomed off. Hector's head bumped and dragged through the dust.

His wife, Andromache, saw it from the wall. She dropped the veil she was wearing. She fainted.

The whole city screamed. It was the darkest day of the war.
