Urban Slang Edition • Book 12

The Sign

Zeus kept the Trojans hyped all night. He was throwing thunderbolts like fireworks.

The next morning, the Trojans advanced on the Greek wall. They were confident.

Polydamas, a smart Trojan captain, pulled Hector aside.

"Yo, look at this bird sign," he said. "A hawk just snatched a dove but the dove fought back. I think it means if we cross the wall, we’re gonna get cooked. Let's stay back and attack with spears."

Hector wasn't feeling it. "You talk too much," he said. "If you're scared, go home. We’re attacking. Zeus is on our side."

The Rush

Hector ordered the charge. But the Greek wall was strong. There was a huge ditch in front of it.

Trojan chariots couldn't get across easily. They got stuck.

So the Trojans had to go on foot. It was chaotic.

•

The Trap at the Gate

One Trojan captain, Asius, saw a gate that was left open. He tried to rush in.

"Easy pickings!" he thought.

Wrong move. It was a trap. Two Greek beasts, Leonteus and Polypoetes, were hiding there.

They jumped him and his squad. It was a slaughter. Asius died fast. The Trojans realized, "Oh, this wall isn't a joke."

Hector's Cannonball

Hector was frustrated. He saw the Greeks holding the line.

He shouted to the captains. "Bring me the biggest rocks you can find!"

He picked up a stone that not even two normal men could lift. He chucked it right at the wooden double doors of the main gate.

BOOM!

The doors shattered. The hinges flew off. The stones crumbled. There was a hole in the defense.

•

Sarpedon's Speech

The Trojans stormed the breach. But the Greeks fought back.

Sarpedon (the King of the Lycians) rallied his men. He gave a legendary speech.

"Listen, bros. Why are we sitting in the back line getting respect? We need to earn it."

"If we die today, at least we die as legends. Let's grab the wall!"

He grabbed his shield and rushed the ramparts. He was fearless.

The Wall War

Sarpedon and Hector climbed the wall. The Greeks were terrified.

Ajax the Great was there, trying to hold the line. He was knocking guys off the wall with stones.

Ajax yelled at Teucer, the archer. "Where have you been? Shoot these guys!"

But Zeus made Teucer's bowstring snap. He shot his brother by accident (lightly).

It was total chaos. The Greeks were fighting for their lives, literally standing on the stones of their own wall.
