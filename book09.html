The Iliad

Urban Slang Edition • Book 9

The Panic Attack

Agamemnon was shook. The Greeks were hiding in their ships, terrified of Hector.

He called a meeting. "I'm done," he said. "Zeus is clearly hating on me. Let's pack up and leave."

Diomedes wasn't having it. "You can run if you want, but I'm staying. The others will agree with me."

Nestor stepped up. "Don't be a coward. But listen, we messed up with Achilles. We need to fix it."

The Peace Offering

Agamemnon realized he was wrong. He decided to send a "goody bag" to Achilles to apologize.

"I'll give him huge gifts," Agamemnon said. "7 cities, gold, and Briseis back. I swear, I didn't touch her. She's still fresh."

He picked a top-tier squad to deliver the message. The smooth talkers:

  • Odysseus (The smart one)
  • Phoenix (Achilles' old mentor)
  • Ajax (The tank)
•

The Tent Visit

The crew walked along the beach. They found Achilles chilling, playing his lyre like he didn't have a care in the world.

Patroclus, his best friend, was there. He served the boys some food and drinks.

Odysseus stood up first. He started his speech.

The Pitch

"Yo, Achilles. The situation is bad. The Trojans are pushing up on our ships. Hector is scary out there."

"Agamemnon knows he messed up. He's sorry. Look at what he’s offering: Infinite gold, horses, and 7 of the finest women."

"Plus, he's giving you one of his daughters. If you come back now, you get a massive dowry. You'd be rich."

The Hard No

Achilles rolled his eyes. "Odysseus, stop with the cap. I don't care about money."

"We all have the same destiny. We all die eventually. Cowards die, heroes die. It doesn't matter."

"Agamemnon took my girl. Nothing he gives me now fixes that disrespect. I'm going home. I want a wife, not war."

•

The Dad Talk

Phoenix started crying. He was the one who raised Achilles when he was a kid.

"Son, don't be like that. Look at the story of Meleager. He got mad at his family and sat out a war. By the time he cooled down, his people had already paid him off with less loot."

"Don't wait until the city is on fire to fight. Take the gifts now."

Achilles was still stubborn. "I like you, Phoenix. You raised me. But I still say no. The anger tastes too good right now."

The Honest Truth

Ajax the Great stood up. He didn't use fancy words. He kept it 100.

"He’s stubborn," Ajax said. "You can offer him the world, he won't take it. He’s holding a grudge too hard."

"Let's just go back and tell the truth. The Greeks are on their own."

The Failure

They walked back. The whole Greek army was waiting.

"So?" they asked. "Is he coming?"

Odysseus shook his head. "Nope. He's too salty."

The army went silent. Everyone was terrified. They spent the night praying and watching Hector light bonfires by their wall.
